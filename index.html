System Queries

What is a ZK PLONK and why is it used? ▼
PLONK is a specific type of Zero-Knowledge proof system (zk-SNARK) that requires only a single, universal trusted setup. We use it so institutions can mathematically prove to auditors that they hold sufficient reserve assets, without revealing individual client balances or specific wallet addresses to the public ledger.
How does Quantum Qustody protect against Shor's Algorithm? ▼
Shor's algorithm can theoretically break RSA and ECC cryptography using a quantum computer. We have transitioned our core infrastructure to Lattice-Based Cryptography (ML-KEM and ML-DSA), which relies on shortest-vector mathematical problems that remain completely intractable even for theoretical quantum mainframes.
Can we self-host the Air-Gapped HSM? ▼
Yes. Under our Sovereign Net tier, large corporations and sovereign entities can self-host the physical Hardware Security Modules in their own datacenters. Our software communicates with your on-premise hardware via strict one-way data diodes.
